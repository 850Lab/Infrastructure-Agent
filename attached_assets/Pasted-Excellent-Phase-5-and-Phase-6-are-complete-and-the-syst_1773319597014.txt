PHASE 7 — OUTCOME EXPLANATION LAYER
==================================================

Goal:
After any logged outcome, the system should clearly explain:

- what outcome was recorded
- what the machine decided
- what changed in the system
- what the next action is
- when that next action is due

Right now the flow engine advances correctly, but the operator should not have to infer the logic.

==================================================
IMPLEMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

After logging any outcome in Focus Mode, show a clear post-log summary panel.

This panel should include:

1. OUTCOME RECORDED
Examples:
- No Answer
- Gave DM Name
- Interested
- Meeting Requested
- Wrong Person

2. SYSTEM ACTION TAKEN
Examples:
- Continued Gatekeeper flow
- Created DM Call flow
- Activated Email flow
- Moved company to Nurture
- Completed current flow
- Scheduled retry

3. NEXT BEST ACTION
Examples:
- Call Mike Turner direct tomorrow at 7:30 AM
- Send Email Step 2 today
- Request LinkedIn connection
- Check back in 90 days

4. WHY THIS ACTION WAS CHOSEN
Examples:
- Gatekeeper provided a direct contact
- Max call attempts reached
- Decision maker requested email follow-up
- No response after multiple touches
- Not relevant right now, moved to nurture

5. SYSTEM STATE CHANGES
Examples:
- Contact added
- DM marked identified
- Gatekeeper flow completed
- New task created in action queue
- Airtable company status updated

This should appear immediately after outcome logging in Focus Mode before auto-advancing, with an option to:
- Continue to next task
- Stay on this company
- Open Company Detail

==================================================
DEFINITIONS
==================================================

Use deterministic definitions, not vague guesses.

Examples:

Companies Touched:
Distinct companies with at least one touch logged in the period

Decision Makers Identified:
Companies or contacts where DM identification occurred from a gatekeeper or contact creation event

Live DM Conversations:
DM call attempts logged with live_answer, interested, meeting_requested, follow_up_scheduled, or similar live-conversation outcomes

Qualified Opportunities:
Companies where the DM confirmed refinery/plant relevance, crew relevance, interest, or moved to an opportunity-ready state

Follow-Ups Scheduled:
Attempts or flows where callback/follow-up date was captured or next action was scheduled intentionally

Warm Accounts:
Companies with at least one live DM conversation and not marked lost

==================================================
UX REQUIREMENTS
==================================================

The KPI area should feel like an operator scoreboard, not generic analytics.

Good UX examples:
- 5-day execution scorecard at top of Today page
- Green/yellow/red indicator versus targets
- Small trend arrows where possible
- “Pipeline is growing” vs “Pipeline is active but shallow” style interpretation text

Example interpretation logic:
- If DMs identified are high but conversations are low → targeting good, connection weak
- If conversations are high but qualified opps are low → message or targeting issue
- If follow-ups are rising steadily → pipeline warming
- If warm accounts are increasing → machine is compounding
